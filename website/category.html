Popular Destinations in London

Discover iconic landmarks and hidden gems in the heart of London.

Tower Bridge

An iconic symbol of London, Tower Bridge is a masterpiece of Victorian engineering, spanning the River Thames with its distinctive twin towers. Visitors can explore its fascinating exhibition to learn about its history or walk across the high-level glass walkway for breathtaking city views. A must-see for its architectural beauty and historical significance.

Big Ben & Houses of Parliament

Big Ben, the nickname for the Great Bell inside the Elizabeth Tower, is a global symbol of London, located next to the grand Houses of Parliament. This Gothic masterpiece is the heart of British politics, offering guided tours to explore its historic chambers. Marvel at the intricate architecture and soak in the rich history of this UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace, the official residence of the British monarch, is renowned for its opulent State Rooms and the daily Changing of the Guard ceremony. Open to visitors in summer, it offers a glimpse into royal life with its stunning art and furnishings. Its central location makes it a perfect starting point for exploring London.

London Eye

The London Eye, a giant Ferris wheel on the South Bank of the River Thames, offers unrivaled panoramic views of London’s skyline, including landmarks like St. Paul’s Cathedral and the Shard. A 30-minute ride provides a unique perspective of the city, especially stunning at sunset. It’s a must-visit for first-time visitors seeking a bird’s-eye view.

British Museum

The British Museum houses a vast collection of world artifacts, including the Rosetta Stone, Egyptian mummies, and Greek sculptures, spanning thousands of years of history. Free to enter, it’s a treasure trove for history enthusiasts, with exhibits that bring global cultures to life. Its iconic Great Court is a stunning architectural highlight.

Covent Garden

Covent Garden is a lively district known for its historic market, vibrant street performers, and eclectic mix of shops, restaurants, and theaters. From boutique shopping to enjoying a meal in a charming courtyard, it’s a cultural hub that captures London’s dynamic spirit. Don’t miss the Royal Opera House or the bustling Apple Market.
